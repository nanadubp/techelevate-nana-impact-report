Coding for Students

Free in-person coding classes for ages 8–14, taught at libraries and partner sites.

What it is

TechElevate’s coding program introduces students to computer science through fun, guided projects. No experience needed — just curiosity.

Quick facts
  • Where: local libraries, after-school programs, nonprofit partners
  • Who: ages 8–14
  • Cost: completely free
  • Length: 8-week and 16-week cohorts
  • Outcome: completion certificates
Curriculum

Powered by Carnegie Mellon University’s Graphic Python program.

1

Create with Shapes

Students start by drawing and designing through code.

2

Animate & Explore

They bring creations to life with motion and interaction.

3

Conditionals & Logic

Students learn if-statements and decision-making.

4

Loops + Final Projects

Learners build games/apps (e.g., Space Invaders-style projects).

Digital Literacy for Seniors

Confidence-building workshops tailored to older adults and community needs.

Why this matters

Many seniors face online risks and frustration navigating today’s digital tools. These sessions make technology safer, clearer, and more empowering.

How we deliver
  • 45-minute sessions, adapted by group needs
  • Hosted with community centers, nonprofits, and retirement homes
  • Hands-on, discussion-friendly teaching style
